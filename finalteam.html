What this assignment is asking your team to do

Use this section like your team’s mini brief before anyone starts coding.

The main goal

Redesign the same website your group used for the midterm analysis. Your new version should feel cleaner, easier to use, more readable, more modern, and better on mobile.

What “good” looks like

  • A polished redesign your group would feel comfortable showing to a future employer
  • Clear improvement over the original site
  • Strong design choices, readable content, and smoother navigation
  • Working responsive layout across desktop, tablet, and phone

Important rules

  • No commercial or pre-made templates as the final design
  • Bootstrap is allowed
  • Use valid HTML and CSS
  • No broken links, no messy file names, no horizontal scrolling

Suggested team timeline

A realistic path so your team is not coding everything the night it is due.

  1. Step 1: Lock the plan Decide what website you are redesigning, review your midterm notes, and agree on the biggest problems to fix.
  2. Step 2: Divide the work Assign roles, decide who handles pages/components, and create a shared folder structure before development starts.
  3. Step 3: Build the redesign Create the improved version using clean HTML/CSS, optional Bootstrap, and a responsive layout.
  4. Step 4: Polish and test Check mobile layout, spacing, readability, broken links, image loading, and visual consistency.
  5. Step 5: Publish and present Put the site online, record your 5-minute video, and prepare your improvement summary.
